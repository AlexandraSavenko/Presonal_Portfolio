Agatha Christie

Best-Selling Novelist: Agatha Christie is considered the best-selling novelist of all time, with over 2 billion copies of her books sold worldwide. Her works have been translated into more than 100 languages.

Prolific Detective Fiction Writer: Christie created two of the most famous detectives in literature: Hercule Poirot, the meticulous Belgian detective, and Miss Marple, the sharp-witted village spinster with a knack for solving mysteries.

Dame of the British Empire: In 1971, Christie was honored with the title of Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE) in recognition of her contributions to literature.
